Program Overview

Island Innovators Bahamas ran its inaugural pilot program in the summer of 2025 across two sessions in Nassau. The program was hosted at The Balmoral Club — an exclusive membership and events venue that provided a vibrant, professional setting for learning, creativity, and collaboration.

Fourteen students ages 9–17 participated across two sessions, engaging in hands-on instruction in music, coding, and robotics. The daily schedule ran from 9:00 AM to 3:00 PM, with structured rotations between subject areas.

The program operated on a tiered access model — offering full scholarships, partial scholarships, and standard enrollment to ensure students from diverse economic backgrounds could participate. Three full scholarships and six partial scholarships were awarded in 2025.

What Students Learned

Coding

  • · Loops, conditionals, and event-driven design
  • · Built personal "About Me" webpages
  • · Designed and programmed original video games
  • · Built restaurant-themed websites from scratch
  • · Used Scratch and CodePen environments

Music

  • · Music theory and chord-building
  • · Rhythm, melody, and sight-reading exercises
  • · Original songs written and performed in groups
  • · Introduction to music production tools
  • · Creative collaboration and live performance

Robotics

  • · Micro:bit programming and physical computing
  • · Circuit design and electronics basics
  • · Logic, sequencing, and control systems
  • · Robot demonstrations and challenge races
  • · Problem-solving through hands-on building

Press Coverage

Featured in the Nassau Tribune

Island Innovators Bahamas was featured in the Nassau Tribune's Weekend Culture section on July 4, 2025. The article highlighted the program's mission, its founder Christopher Hilton, and its impact on Nassau youth.

"My vision for Island Innovators was never meant to be just a one-time summer programme; I've always envisioned it growing into something bigger, more sustainable, and more accessible across The Bahamas." — Christopher Hilton

Looking Ahead to 2026

Building on the 2025 pilot, Island Innovators Bahamas 2026 will run July 13–24 at St. John's College in Nassau. The program will deepen its three-track model — Coding, Music, and Robotics — with a selective cohort of 12–20 students and a final showcase on July 25 at the New Providence Community Center.
