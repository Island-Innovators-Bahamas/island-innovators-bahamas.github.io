2026 Program

Create. Produce. Perform.

One focused program. Performance and production developed together. Students leave having created and developed work they can perform, present, or continue building beyond the program — along with a clearer understanding of their musicianship, discipline, and creative process. Through that work, students begin to define their own artistic identity.

An Island Innovator doesn't just attend — they produce, perform, and leave having met a standard. Students who complete this program become part of a growing cohort of serious young Bahamian artists with continued access to opportunities as the program expands. This is not a program you attend. It's one you're selected into.

What You'll Do

  • →Build real musicianship — ear training, theory, and performance that carries beyond the program
  • →Work with professional music production software and tools
  • →Complete original work — composed, produced, or both — ready to present
  • →Perform your work publicly at the final showcase — the moment everything builds toward

The Founder

Built by a Bahamian, for Bahamians

Christopher Hilton is a sophomore at Oberlin College & Conservatory, where he studies Jazz Bass Performance under Gerald Cannon — a faculty member at both Oberlin and Juilliard, alongside a minor in Computer Science.

After years on electric bass, he began the upright bass two months before his Oberlin prescreening, and auditioned live after five months of study.

Island Innovators Bahamas was built in his dorm room during his first year at Oberlin to make that kind of development possible without leaving home. The program reflects that approach — where music and technology aren't separate paths, but tools students use to create something real.

For Parents

Frequently Asked Questions

```
Students should come in with some foundation in music — formal or self-taught. This program is built to push students who are already engaged, not introduce the subject from scratch. We look for curiosity, commitment, and a genuine interest in music over credentials or accolades.
Ages 11–17, currently in Grades 6–12. We accept students entering any grade in the 2026–27 school year. In exceptional cases, applicants slightly outside this range may be considered based on fit and readiness.
Students arrive at 9:00 AM and the day runs until 3:00 PM, Monday–Friday. Each day blends instruction, hands-on project work, and collaboration — all building toward the final public showcase.
Need-based financial assistance is available in select cases as part of admissions. Tuition is $500 — 2026 Founding Cohort rate. Strong applicants are not turned away for financial reasons alone.
We typically accept 12–20 students per cohort. Every application is reviewed carefully. We look for genuine curiosity, a specific reason for wanting to attend, commitment to completing a project, and some existing engagement with music.
The 2026 program takes place in Nassau, Bahamas. Final venue details will be shared with accepted students before the program begins.
```
