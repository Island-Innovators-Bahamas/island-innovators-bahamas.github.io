What You'll Build

Choose Your Track

The 2025 pilot explored music, coding, and robotics — not as separate subjects, but as connected ways of thinking: building, listening, problem-solving, creating. In 2026, the program goes deeper into one of those threads. Students work at the intersection of music performance and music production, developing craft and technology together — and leaving with original work they've performed publicly.

Coding

Developers

Learn to think logically, break problems apart, and bring ideas to life through code. Students work on real projects — designing, building, and iterating on something they can call their own. No prior experience required; what matters is curiosity and the drive to create.

You're Working Toward

A working project — built in code, from scratch, ready to present at the showcase.

Music

Artists

Music is both a discipline and a language. This track covers performance and composition — students develop their ear, build musicianship, study theory, and explore creation tools. Whether your interest is instruments, production, or songwriting, this track meets you where you are and pushes you forward.

You're Working Toward

Performed and/or composed musical work, presented live at the final showcase.

Robotics

Builders

Build something that moves, senses, and responds. Students learn physical computing, work with circuits and microcontrollers, and solve real engineering problems with their hands. This is the track for students who want to understand how things actually work — and then make them work better.

You're Working Toward

A working robotic or physical computing system, demonstrated at the final showcase.

Every track ends at the same place: the final showcase. Students present their finished work — live — to family, mentors, and the community. Real projects. Real pride.

Why This Is Different

Nothing Else Like This Here

Music. Coding. Robotics.
Brought together — intentionally — in one program built for Nassau.

Rooted Here

No other program in Nassau offers music, coding, and robotics together in one selective youth intensive. Most alternatives separate disciplines — or require leaving the country entirely. Island Innovators Bahamas delivers a complete, multidisciplinary program locally. The nearest comparable alternatives require a plane ticket.

Small by Design

12–20 students per cohort. Every student receives genuine mentorship and substantive feedback. This is a program where your work is seen and your growth is intentional — not one where you disappear into a crowd.

Outcomes Over Activities

No busywork. No filler. Students build toward real, showcase-ready outcomes from day one. Every session has a purpose. Every day advances the work. The program ends with something to show for it.

About the Founder

Built by a Bahamian, for Bahamians

Christopher Hilton is the founder of Island Innovators Bahamas, an initiative created to expand access to creative and technical opportunity for young people across The Bahamas.

He is a sophomore at Oberlin College and Conservatory, where he studies Jazz Bass Performance and Computer Science — an intersection that reflects the philosophy behind Island Innovators Bahamas. Originally founded during his first year at Oberlin, the initiative continues to evolve alongside his work at the intersection of music, technology, and mentorship.

As both a musician and a student of technology, Christopher has never experienced music and coding as separate paths. From an early curiosity about how things work — whether through sound, structure, or code — he developed a way of thinking rooted in creativity, problem-solving, and exploration. Island Innovators Bahamas was built from that perspective: bringing music, coding, robotics, and Bahamian youth into the same space to create something more meaningful than traditional learning environments.

His approach is shaped not only by what he has studied, but by how he has learned. Through mentorship, practice, and trial and error, Christopher developed a belief that growth happens in environments where students are given both support and space — where they are encouraged to ask questions, struggle through ideas, and discover solutions for themselves. That belief now defines how Island Innovators Bahamas is designed: as a space where students can experiment, collaborate, and build confidence through real work.

More than anything, Christopher created Island Innovators Bahamas to open doors. He understands that access to the right tools, guidance, and environment can fundamentally change how young people see themselves. The goal is not only to teach skills, but to create moments where students begin to recognize their own potential — often in ways they hadn't considered before.

What started as a summer initiative has grown into something larger. Christopher is building Island Innovators Bahamas with the long-term vision of expanding opportunity, mentorship, and creative access for Bahamian youth — starting with a single initiative, but not ending there.

The 2025 pilot marked the beginning. The 2026 program continues that work with a more focused and intentional experience, as Island Innovators Bahamas grows into a platform for long-term impact.

For Parents

What You Need to Know

Students should come in with some foundation in music — whether formal or self-taught. This program is designed to push students who are already engaged, not introduce the subject from scratch. We look for curiosity, commitment, and a genuine interest in music over credentials or accolades.
Ages 11–17, currently in Grades 6–12. We accept students entering any grade in the 2026–27 school year. In exceptional cases, applicants slightly outside this range may be considered based on fit and readiness.
Students arrive at 9:00 AM and the day runs until 3:00 PM, Monday–Friday. Each day blends instruction, hands-on project work, and collaboration. The program is designed to build toward a final public showcase at the end.
Need-based financial assistance is available in select cases and considered as part of the admissions process. We aim to ensure that strong applicants are able to attend regardless of financial circumstance. Tuition and financial assistance details will be released at the official launch in May.
We typically accept 12–20 students per cohort. Every application is reviewed carefully. We look for genuine curiosity, a specific reason for wanting to attend, and commitment to completing a project. Students should have some existing engagement with music — the program is built to develop and challenge that, not start from zero.
The 2026 program will take place in Nassau, Bahamas. Final venue details will be shared at launch and confirmed with accepted students.
