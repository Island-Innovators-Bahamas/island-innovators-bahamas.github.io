What You'll Build

Choose Your Track

Pick one. Go deep. Every day moves you closer to something real — a finished project you'll be proud to show.

Coding

Learn to think logically, break problems apart, and bring ideas to life through code. Students work on real projects — designing, building, and iterating on something they can call their own. No prior experience required; what matters is curiosity and the drive to create.

You're Working Toward

A working project — built in code, from scratch, ready to present at the showcase.

Music

Music is both a discipline and a language. This track covers performance and composition — students develop their ear, build musicianship, study theory, and explore creation tools. Whether your interest is instruments, production, or songwriting, this track meets you where you are and pushes you forward.

You're Working Toward

Performed and/or composed musical work, presented live at the final showcase.

Robotics

Build something that moves, senses, and responds. Students learn physical computing, work with circuits and microcontrollers, and solve real engineering problems with their hands. This is the track for students who want to understand how things actually work — and then make them work better.

You're Working Toward

A working robotic or physical computing system, demonstrated at the final showcase.

Every track ends at the same place: the final showcase. Students present their finished work — live — to family, mentors, and the community. Real projects. Real pride.

Why This Is Different

Nothing Else Like This Here

Coding, music, and robotics — together, intentionally, in a single youth-centered program built for Nassau.

Rooted Here

No other program in Nassau offers coding, music, and robotics together in one selective youth intensive. Most alternatives separate disciplines — or require leaving the country entirely. Island Innovators Bahamas delivers a complete, multidisciplinary program locally. The nearest comparable alternatives require a plane ticket.

Small by Design

12–20 students per cohort. Every student receives genuine mentorship and substantive feedback. This is a program where your work is seen and your growth is intentional — not one where you disappear into a crowd.

Outcomes Over Activities

No busywork. No filler. Students build toward real, showcase-ready outcomes from day one. Every session has a purpose. Every day advances the work. The program ends with something to show for it.

About the Founder

Built by a Bahamian, for Bahamians

Christopher Hilton is a Bahamian from Nassau and a sophomore at Oberlin College & Conservatory studying Jazz Double Bass Performance and Computer Science. He created Island Innovators Bahamas as a freshman, starting it in his dorm room with the idea that music, technology, and Bahamian youth belonged in the same room together.

The 2025 pilot proved it worked. The 2026 program is what comes next.

What You Need to Know

We're not only looking for the most experienced applicants — we're looking for students who show curiosity, initiative, and a willingness to build something meaningful. Prior experience in coding, music, or robotics is welcome, but what matters most is a student's drive to grow across disciplines and contribute seriously to the cohort.
Ages 11–17, currently in Grades 6–12. We accept students entering any grade in the 2026–27 school year.
Students arrive at 9:00 AM and the day runs until 3:00 PM, Monday–Friday, July 13–24. Each day blends instruction, hands-on project work, and collaboration. Every day is intentional and builds toward the final showcase on July 25.
Yes. In 2025 we provided 3 full scholarships and 6 partial scholarships, funded by the Government of The Bahamas, Island Game, and the Bonner Scholars Program. Need-based financial assistance is available in limited cases. We aim to ensure that strong applicants are able to attend regardless of financial circumstance. If tuition is a barrier, please indicate that on your application — we review assistance requests as part of the admissions process.
We typically accept 12–16 students per cohort. Every application is reviewed carefully. We look for genuine curiosity, a specific reason for wanting to attend, demonstrated initiative, and commitment to completing a project. Students may come in with strength in one area — what matters is their willingness to grow across disciplines and engage seriously with the program.
